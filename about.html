About me

Hey there,
My name is Pierre Collier. What you are viewing on your screen right now is my first step. The first step to becoming a fully-fledged front-end developer, that is.

So, why Pierrot Kwame?
Pierrot is a reference to the nickname my mother would sometimes use for me when I was a child. Simply put it is the French diminutive form of my first given name. It is also the name of a sad clown, the victim of unrequited love, a common pantomime character in commedia dell'arte. Hopefully, this was nothing my mother had in mind.
Kwame is my second given name, a reference to Ghanaian president Kwame Nkrumah. Nkrumah was the first elected president in a newly independent nation after colonialism. A person held in high regard by my parents due to his socialist ideology and prominent ideas of an united Africa. I can appreciate the symbolism in giving me the name, but I am also aware of Nkrumah being anything but a perfect leader. As politicians seldom are.

The blog posts here are of course not written by me. They are rather an attempt to present me and my interests through excerpts from Wikipedia.

If you would like to know more of me in a professional capacity, on LinkedIn is where you can find what I did before my intention to change careers. You can find me and any repos of mine on GitHub. Also where this site is hosted.
